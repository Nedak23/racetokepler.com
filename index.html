Four decks. Twenty city-ships. One star chart. Ready your table.

Race to Kepler is a deck-building board game about the last ships out of a dying Earth. World powers have hollowed their cities into starcraft and set course for Kepler-452b. Hire crews from four classes, keep your ship's energy burning, and bid against rivals for the ancient Titans that bend the rules of the race. First ship to cross twenty star systems wins — and not a moment before.

Gameplay Features

Race Across Twenty Systems

Victory isn't vague. The chart is twenty systems long and the first ship to Kepler takes the prize. Every jump matters. Falling behind is survivable — up to a point. Falling two jumps behind is not.

Classes

Engineers

The ones who keep the lights on. Engineers are your ship's nervous system — they route energy, stabilize the reactor, and make sure every upgrade you bolt on actually fires. On the table, they're the class that scales: each Engineer you add multiplies the work of the rest.

Choose the Engineers if you enjoy tempo, tight combos, and decks that only get stronger turn over turn.

Miners

Grubby hands, full pockets. Miners pry the money out of whatever's in front of them — crew, asteroid, abandoned station. They're slow to start and impossible to catch once they're up to speed. The economic class; pick them when you want to out-bid the whole table for every Titan on the market.

Choose the Miners if you enjoy the long snowball, deep setups, and out-bidding the whole table once your engine is humming.

Scientists

The ones who read the rules and then rewrite them. Scientists turn discards into draws, turn Titans into tech, and turn whole turns inside-out. Nothing in Race to Kepler is quite as satisfying as a Scientist combo that nobody at the table saw coming.

Choose the Scientists if you enjoy card manipulation, tech rushes, and pulling a winning plan together from the bottom of the deck.

Smugglers

Working the cracks between the rules. Smugglers disrupt, cut, steal, and disappear. A full Smuggler deck is a series of small insults that add up to somebody else losing the race. Not the easiest class to teach — but if you want to be the table's problem, this is your crew.

Choose the Smugglers if you enjoy hidden plays, targeted disruption, and forcing every opponent to change their plan mid-turn.
